Getting started

1
Upload your resume Drag and drop or click the upload area. PDF, Word (.docx), and plain text (.txt) files all work. You can upload up to 3 versions at once — useful if you have different resumes for different roles.
2
Paste the job description Copy the full job posting — title, responsibilities, requirements — and paste it into the job description box. The more detail you include, the better the tailoring.
3
Pick a tone Choose how you want the final resume to read. "Professional" is a safe default. "Impactful" emphasizes results and numbers. "Concise" trims to essentials. "Executive / senior" uses leadership-level language.
4
Click "Tailor my resume" The AI panel will read your resume and the job posting. This usually takes 20–40 seconds when using the full AI. A progress message will appear while it works.
5
Read the panel results Each reviewer gives you a score, highlights strengths, flags gaps, and suggests specific improvements. Scroll down to see what changed and preview the tailored resume.
6
Download your resume Choose PDF or Word format and click Download. The file is ready to submit immediately.

What the review panel does

Four AI reviewers each read your resume through a different lens. Their job is to catch what you'd miss if you only read your own resume.

Technical Recruiter

Reads your resume the way a recruiter skimming dozens of applications would. Looks for clear job titles, relevant experience, readable formatting, and whether your background matches the role at a glance. A low score here means the resume won't make it past the first filter.

ATS / Keyword Specialist

Most companies use Applicant Tracking Systems that scan resumes for keywords before a human ever sees them. This reviewer checks whether the right skills, tools, and phrases from the job description appear in your resume — and flags what's missing.

Hiring Manager

Reads as the person who actually needs to fill this role. Focuses on whether your experience shows real impact, whether your accomplishments are specific and measurable, and whether you sound like someone who can do this job — not just someone who has done similar jobs.

Professional Resume Editor

Checks the writing itself: clarity, conciseness, action verbs, consistency, and anything that sounds vague or overly generic. Flags filler phrases ("responsible for," "helped with") and suggests sharper language.

What the numbers mean

Panel match score (before tailoring) — the overall score shown at the top is how well your original resume matched the job, before any changes. It's a starting-point diagnostic, not a final grade. The tailored resume is always stronger than this number suggests.

Individual reviewer scores — each reviewer's score (shown top-right of their card) reflects how well your resume performed on that reviewer's specific criteria. Scores under 60 usually mean there's meaningful room to improve on that dimension.

Strengths — things your resume already does well for this role. Keep them.

Gaps — things the job requires that your resume doesn't address. The tailored resume will try to surface relevant experience that covers these.

Suggestions — specific rewrites or additions the reviewer recommends.

Common problems

"Are you signed in?" error or page reloads unexpectedly

Your session has expired. Refresh the page and sign in again with Google. If the sign-in button isn't showing, make sure cookies are enabled in your browser and you're not in private/incognito mode.

Timeout error ("Something went wrong" after a long wait)

The AI panel can take 1–3 minutes in local Claude plan mode. If it times out, try again — occasional timeouts are normal. If it keeps failing, make sure you're signed into Claude Code (claude in a terminal) and raise CLAUDE_CODE_TIMEOUT in your .env.

File won't upload

Only PDF, Word (.docx), and plain text (.txt) files are supported. Make sure the file isn't password-protected. Files larger than a few MB may be rejected — try saving as plain text if the PDF is very large. You can upload up to 3 files total.

Download not working

The download button appears after a successful tailor run. If nothing happens when you click it, check your browser's download settings and make sure pop-ups aren't being blocked. Try switching between PDF and Word format — one may work when the other doesn't.

Frequently asked questions

Is my resume stored anywhere?

No. Your resume is processed in memory during the session and never written to disk or saved to a database. When your session ends, it's gone.

What file types are supported?

PDF, Word (.docx), and plain text (.txt). If your resume is in another format, copy the text and save it as a .txt file.

Can I upload more than one resume?

Yes — up to 3 files at once. This is useful if you have different versions of your resume (one focused on technical skills, one on leadership, etc.). The panel will consider all of them and draw from whichever content fits the job best.

What does the score mean?

The panel match score reflects how well your original resume matched the job posting — before any tailoring. Think of it as the starting point. The tailored resume the app produces is always stronger than the score suggests, because the score measures the input, not the output.

Will it make up things that aren't on my resume?

No. TailorCV only rephrases and reorders content that's already there. It will highlight relevant experience more prominently and sharpen the language, but it won't invent jobs, skills, or accomplishments you don't have.

How much does it cost to run?

Each tailor run makes ~5 calls through your local Claude plan (4 reviewers in parallel + 1 synthesis). These count against your Claude Pro/Max plan's usage limits, not a separate API charge.

Can I customize the reviewers?

Yes. Click "Manage reviewers" at the top of the app to edit any of the four default reviewers or add your own. You can write custom instructions for any persona — for example, a "Startup Culture Fit" reviewer or one focused on your specific industry.
